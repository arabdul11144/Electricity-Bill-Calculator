Sri Lanka has recently implemented significant changes to its electricity tariffs as of July 2024. The Public Utilities Commission of Sri Lanka (PUCSL) approved a notable reduction in electricity rates across various consumer categories. For domestic users, the tariff has been reduced by approximately 27%, with specific reductions across different consumption brackets. For instance, the rate for the 0-30 unit bracket was reduced from Rs. 8 to Rs. 6, and the 31-60 unit bracket saw a drop from Rs. 20 to Rs. 9. Additionally, religious places of worship experienced a 30% reduction, while hotels and industries benefited from a 25% decrease in their electricity tariffs. This adjustment is part of broader efforts by the government to ease the financial burden on consumers and support various sectors, following previous increases earlier in the year aimed at stabilizing the energy sector's finances.
